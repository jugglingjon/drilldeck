Dynamic Stretches

Dynamic stretching is designed to move joints and muscles in repetitive, slightly challenging motions over a set amount of time. This movement helps prepare the body to exercise by increasing your heart rate and getting your muscles ready to work.

Before upper body workouts:

  • Arm circles: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart and hold arms out to the sides, palms down, at shoulder height. Move your arms in circles, first clockwise and then counter-clockwise. Do about three to five of these arm circles in each direction.
  • T push-ups: Start out in the push-up position, and then lower yourself down towards the ground. As you push back up, extend your right arm toward the sky while keeping your left arm stable and your hips from moving down or up. Bring your arm back to the starting position, do another push-up, and then repeat with the left arm.

Before lower body workouts:

  • Lunges: Step forward with one leg, lunging down into a 90-degree angle. Bring back to standing position and step forward into a lunch on the opposite side. Alternate back and forth.
  • High kicks: Kick one leg out straight in front of you up to waist height to stretch out your hamstrings. Kick your other leg up high. Repeat, alternating kicks between both legs. Keep your arms at your sides and focus on getting each kick higher than the last.

Before core workouts:

  • Torso twists: Stand straight, keeping your hips still, and twist your upper body from left to right. Do about two to three of twists to each side within five minutes of the start of the event.
  • Ab contractions: Standing up, squeeze to tighten your abdominal muscles for three seconds and then release. Repeat.
  • Hip stretch: Start in the push-up position and bring your right foot up to your right hand while keeping your hips down and lower back flat. Take your left hand, twist to your left while extending your arm and reaching toward the sky. Come back to the starting push-up position and repeat on the other side.
  • Hip extensor: Hinge forward at your hips. Raise your right foot and bend your right knee in front of you while swinging your left arm forward as you would when running. From there, maintain the same lean as you quickly stretch your right leg behind you. At the same time, swing your right arm forward and your left arm back. Return your knee in front of you and repeat with the other leg.

Before full body workouts:

  • Jumping jacks: Get your blood pumping by doing some good, old-fashioned jumping jacks. High knees: Lift one knee up towards your chest before stepping back down. Lift your other knee up to your chest and back down. Repeat rapidly, alternating high knees on both legs while moving your arms back and forth like you’re running.

Static Stretches

Static stretching involves holding a muscle or joint in an extended position for 30-60 seconds. This type of stretching helps by relaxing the body part and may increase the flexibility of the muscle’s tissue.

After upper body workouts:

  • Chest stretch: Stand While standing,tall with your feet hip-width apart and shoulders, hips and ankles in alignment. Lift your chest up and out as you roll your shoulders back and pull your shoulder blades together. Lift your hands toward the ceiling until you feel a stretch in your chest. Hold for 30 to 60 seconds.
  • Shoulder Cross-body arms stretch: Relax your shoulders, bring one arm across your body, and hold it with the other arm just above the elbow, pulling gently toward your body.
  • Shoulder joint stretch: Hold a towel tightly in front of you with both hands, and then slowly raise your hands with your arms straightened out until the towel ends up behind you. When holding the towel behind you, make sure your hands are close together. Hold for about 30 seconds.

After lower body workouts:

  • Hamstrings: Lie on your back and bend both knees. Pull one leg at a time in towards your chest. Do this with your knee bent and then try it with keep your leg straight.
  • Quadriceps: Standing and holding onto something for balance, bring your right heel towards your butt. Lie on your side. Bend your top leg, bringing your heel towards your butt. Grasp your foot and pull gently, bringing your foot closer to your butt. Hold and then repeat on the other side.

After core workouts:

  • Cobra pose: Lie prone on the floor with your legs extended behind you and the tops of your feet on the ground. Place your hands under your shoulders, keeping your elbows in. close to your torso. Press your feet, thighs, pelvis and hands into the floor as you straighten your arms and lift your head toward the ceiling.. Hold the pose for 15 to 30 seconds.
  • Overhead stretch: Standing straight up, lace your fingers together and open your palms to the ceiling. Keep your shoulders down as you extend your arms up.Groin stretch: Sit on the floor with the soles of your feet touching. While holding your toes, lean your upper body forward, bending from the hips.

  • Calf stretch: Sit with one leg outstretched. Grab the toes of that foot. Slowly pull the toes toward you until you feel stretch in calf muscles. If you cannot reach your toes, use a towel to pull toes back.flex your toes to stretch your calf or use a towel to pull toes back.
  • Lower back stretch: Lie on your back with knees bent. Hug your shins and slowly pull your knees up to the chest until you feel the stretch in your lower back.
  • Hip flexor stretch: Standing and holding onto something for balance, place your right ankle above your left knee, creating a 90-degree angle with your right leg. Sit back, moving your hips toward the ground. Repeat on other side.
